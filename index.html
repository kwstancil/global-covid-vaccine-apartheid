Global Covid Vaccine Apartheid

Authored by: Kenny Stancil @New Maps Plus, Univ. of Kentucky, Dept. of Geography

This map depicts unequal access to Covid-19 vaccines. Of the nearly 2.9 billion doses that had been administered as of June 25, 2021, just 8.29 million, or 0.03%, shots were given in low-income countries, a manifestation of global health injustice that progressives around the world have dubbed "vaccine apartheid." Oxfam estimated recently that at the current pace, it would take impoverished nations 57 years to fully inoculate their populations. One of the key factors driving the inequitable distribution of vaccines, according to public health experts, is an articifial scarcity of doses caused by intellectual property barriers. In October 2020, India and South Africa introduced a proposal to suspend coronavirus-related IP for the duration of the pandemic. Their motion to temporarily waive the Trade-Related Aspects of Intellectual Property Rights (TRIPS) Agreement has been supported by more than 100 of the World Trade Organization's member nations. However, a handful of mostly rich countries continues to side with Big Pharma, which has profited from its monopolization of publicly funded knowledge, in their opposition to waiving patent protections. Sharing vaccine recipes and transfering technology would increase the worldwide production of doses. Epidemiologists have warned that failing to boost global vaccine manufacturing will prolong the pandemic, threatening millions of additional lives and livelihoods as the virus is given more time to mutate into poentially vaccine-resistant variants.

Tools used in this project

  • I used QGIS 3.18 on Windows 10 to create GeoJSON files containing national vaccination rates as well as governments' positions on the TRIPS waiver proposal under negotiation at the WTO.
  • The map is displayed on a Carto web map.
  • Data sources: Information about the number of doses administered in each country (as of 6/25/2021) came courtesy of Our World in Data - see Mathieu, E., Ritchie, H., Ortiz-Ospina, E. et al. A global database of COVID-19 vaccinations. Nat Hum Behav (2021); Information about whether countries support or oppose the TRIPS waiver proposal was supplied by Doctors Without Borders; Finally, Natural Earth provided spatial data.
